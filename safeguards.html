Safeguards and commitment policy

Normativity tools are pilots for structured inquiry. They should never turn confidence, coherence, or market signals into public proof, social pressure, or binding authority over other people.

Policy status

Pilot policy Updated May 28, 2026

Any production deployment should publish a fuller moderation, dispute, and data-retention policy before collecting sensitive records.

Optional follow-through under uncertainty

After a dialogue, a participant may choose to create a limited, revocable follow-through plan if their own post-session confidence exceeds a threshold they selected in advance. Crossing that threshold does not mean the proposition is settled, morally proven, or binding on anyone else.

What it allows

A participant may act on their current best view in a bounded way while remaining open to revision. Plans should name the scope, duration, privacy level, review path, and exit conditions.

What it forbids

  • No public claim that a threshold proves moral truth.
  • No shaming for declining, revising, or revoking a plan.
  • No hidden expansion beyond the participant's stated scope.
  • No use against non-participants or third parties.

Dialogue method policy

Dialogue is for improving understanding, not winning a debate or manufacturing consent.

Steelman first

State your counterpart's strongest case before criticizing it.

Sealed updates

Post-session confidence updates should be submitted independently where possible.

Reason quality

Participants should distinguish case judgments, principles, background theories, and facts.

Review and revocation

Ground Review response
Coercion or pressure Pause the plan, remove public visibility, and review consent conditions.
Ambiguity Clarify scope before any accountability claim continues.
Changed evidence Permit revision or revocation with a short reason record.
Impossibility or disproportionate burden Adjust the plan, end it, or move to a lower-burden version.
Privacy risk Redact, export, delete, or move records local-only where feasible.
