Team and role disclosures

Normativity is currently presented as a research project. A production-quality public institution should publish named responsible people, review roles, conflicts, and escalation paths before collecting sensitive pilot data or claiming institutional authority.

Roles that should be named before broader pilots

These are the accountability roles a visitor should be able to identify before trusting sensitive deliberation, belief-graph, or commitment workflows.

Role Current public status Minimum disclosure before broader pilot
Research lead Not yet named on the public site. Name, affiliation, authorship policy, and correction responsibility.
Prototype maintainer Not yet named on the public site. Name or organization responsible for data handling, security, and uptime claims.
Governance reviewer Not yet named on the public site. Review scope, appeal path, and conflicts of interest.
Privacy contact Shared inbox only. Named or role-based owner for export, deletion, retention, and correction requests.

Before claiming institutional trust

Publish people

List named authors, maintainers, reviewers, board members, or explain any anonymity policy with substitute trust signals.

Publish conflicts

Disclose funding, affiliations, governance rights, and material incentives that could shape agenda-setting.

Publish escalation paths

Make correction, data, moderation, and appeal paths visible from every sensitive tool surface.
