Tools as research methods

The tools are experimental infrastructure for testing methods. They are not public verdicts, not automated moral authorities, and not binding systems for anyone else.

WRE method

Compare judgments, principles, and background theories, then record tensions and repair options.

  • Use it for: finding tensions across cases, principles, and assumptions.
  • Known limits: conflict detection is not proof of moral truth.
  • Data handling: demo sessions stay local unless a user exports or opts into sync.
Read the walkthrough

Dialogue method

Use paired disagreement, steelmanning, and sealed post-session updates to reduce performative debate.

  • Use it for: clarifying cruxes and recording post-session updates.
  • Known limits: agreement is not required and pressure is out of scope.
  • Data handling: public sharing requires consent and review paths.
Read the policy

Credence markets method

Visualize uncertainty with non-financial signals, explanatory factors, and weekly snapshots.

  • Use it for: comparing uncertainty and reasons across a bounded question.
  • Known limits: estimates can be biased, sparse, or socially gamed.
  • Data handling: public examples use labeled illustrative data.

Optional follow-through under uncertainty

After a dialogue, a participant may choose to create a limited, revocable follow-through plan if their own post-session confidence exceeds a threshold they selected in advance. Crossing that threshold does not mean the proposition is settled, morally proven, or binding on anyone else.

What the threshold means

It only means the participant may want to act on their current best view in a bounded way while remaining open to revision.

Read commitment policy

Required safeguards

  • Participant-chosen threshold.
  • No threshold equals truth.
  • Bounded scope and duration.
  • Revocation, appeal, and human review.
  • Privacy by default and data minimization.
  • No public shaming or reputational pressure.
