Methods and known limits

Normativity methods are designed to improve inquiry, not to automate moral authority.

Method commitments

  • Separate empirical, conceptual, and normative claims before arguing.
  • Record confidence changes and reasons without treating confidence as truth.
  • Make disagreement structure visible through examples and revision logs.
  • Publish corrections and limits where public claims change.

Known limits

  • Moral claims often lack clean external resolution conditions.
  • Participants can Goodhart scores, markets, or coherence measures.
  • Public uptake depends on trust, power, and messenger diversity.
  • Tool outputs can clarify uncertainty but cannot settle moral truth alone.

Normativity Research publishes revisable documents and method notes for moral inquiry under uncertainty.
