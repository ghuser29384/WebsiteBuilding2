About Normativity

Normativity is a research project for improving moral disagreement before harmful norms harden into institutions. It builds method notes, example tools, and safeguards for moral inquiry under uncertainty.

Current status

Research and prototype stage. Public pages should be read as method proposals and labeled examples, not production services.

Mission

Help people and institutions reason better under moral uncertainty by making disagreement structured, uncertainty visible, and action-facing commitments bounded and reversible.

Research

Publish revisable documents on moral discovery, recognition failures, and public judgment.

Tools

Prototype WRE workflows, credence mapping, and dialogue methods with labeled example data.

Safeguards

Keep privacy, revocation, appeal, and anti-coercion rules visible before broader deployment.

People and governance

The public site does not yet publish a complete named team, board, or funder list. That is a known trust gap, not something hidden behind product language. See governance for the current disclosure posture and pilot gates.
